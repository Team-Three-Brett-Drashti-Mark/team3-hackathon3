# User Personas

---

## 👩‍💻 Persona 1 — The Struggling Student

**Name:** Marcus, 24
**Role:** Cohort student, 6 weeks in

### Goals
- Get unstuck quickly without feeling embarrassed asking basic questions
- Understand *why* something works, not just that it does
- Keep up with the cohort pace despite gaps in his background

### Frustrations
- Feels lost when curriculum materials are scattered across platforms
- Asks Google and gets answers that contradict what the instructor taught
- Worries the assistant will just give him the answer and he won't actually learn
- Shuts down when he doesn't know what question to even ask

---

## 🚀 Persona 2 — The High Performer

**Name:** Priya, 28
**Role:** Cohort student, top of her cohort

### Goals
- Go deeper than the curriculum when she finishes early
- Validate her own reasoning before moving on
- Use the assistant as a thinking partner, not a crutch

### Frustrations
- Gets bored when the assistant over-explains things she already knows
- Wants to probe edge cases but hits a wall when the assistant stays too surface-level
- Frustrated when she can't tell if her approach is on the right track without being told outright

---

## 🗂️ Persona 3 — The Administrator

**Name:** Sandra, 42
**Role:** Program Operations Manager, non-technical

### Goals
- Know whether students are actually using the system
- Spot struggling students before they fall too far behind
- Keep the curriculum up to date without filing a ticket with engineering

### Frustrations
- Dashboards that require technical knowledge to interpret
- No visibility into what students are confused about until it's too late
- Fears being dependent on a developer every time a PDF needs to be updated
- Worried she won't be able to tell if the assistant is "cheating" on behalf of students

---

## 🎓 Persona 4 — The Instructor

**Name:** Dev, 36
**Role:** Lead Instructor, built most of the curriculum

### Goals
- Trust that the assistant reflects his curriculum accurately, not a generic LLM
- Use student question patterns to improve future lessons
- Stay out of the system's day-to-day without it breaking

### Frustrations
- Curriculum materials are inconsistent — some are polished, some are rough drafts
- Worried the assistant will misrepresent a concept he taught a specific way
- Doesn't want to babysit the tool but also doesn't fully trust it unsupervised
