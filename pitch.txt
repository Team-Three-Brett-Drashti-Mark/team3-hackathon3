DRASHTI — OPEN (Problem + User) ~1.5 min
Cohort-based programs generate a lot of curriculum, but once it’s delivered, it becomes hard to actually use.
Students hit a point where they’re stuck, and they don’t know where to go back in the material to figure it out. Everything is spread across PDFs, markdown files, quizzes, and rubrics. So instead of learning, they either spin their wheels or go looking for answers somewhere else.
At the same time, instructors have no visibility into what’s actually happening. They see final submissions, but not where students struggled to get there.
So the two users we’re solving for are students who need help during learning, and admins who need visibility into that process.

BRETT — SOLUTION + UX ~2 min
We’re building a private learning assistant that sits directly on top of the program’s curriculum.
Students can ask questions in natural language, but instead of giving answers, the system guides them. It points them to the right material, asks follow-up questions, and helps them think through the problem.
From the student side, the experience is simple. They ask a question, and the system responds with guidance. It might point them to a specific lesson, ask a clarifying question, or walk them through how to think about the problem.
From the admin side, we surface what’s actually happening. You can see what topics are being asked the most, where students are getting stuck, where the system is blocking answer-seeking behavior, and which parts of the curriculum are being heavily referenced.
Everything is designed to be usable by a non-technical user.

MARK — ARCHITECTURE + INPUT/OUTPUT ~2.5 min
We’re designing this as a Bronze, Silver, Gold pipeline.
At the Bronze layer, we take the raw curriculum as it exists. PDFs, markdown, quizzes, and rubrics.
At the Silver layer, we process that content. We extract text, clean it, remove duplicates, and break it into smaller chunks. Then we add metadata like week, topic, and assignment type so it becomes structured and searchable.
At the Gold layer, that content is stored in a vector database and connected to a retriever.
When a student asks a question, we retrieve the most relevant curriculum first. Then we pass that through a guardrail layer that enforces the rule of no direct answers.
Only after that does it go to the LLM, which generates a guided response based on the retrieved material.(pause for Brett to change slide)
In terms of inputs and outputs, the inputs are raw curriculum and student questions.
The output is not a direct answer. It’s a guided response tied back to the curriculum, along with system logs that feed into admin insights.

DRASTHI — SCOPE + CLOSE ~1 min
For MVP, we’re focusing on the core system.
We’re building ingestion for PDFs and markdown, implementing chunking and metadata tagging, setting up vector search and retrieval, adding a guardrail layer, and building logging for admin visibility.
We’re intentionally not focusing on things like full UI polish or advanced analytics yet. The goal is to prove the core system works.
This isn’t just a chatbot.
It’s a pipeline that takes messy curriculum, makes it searchable, and controls how the model interacts with students so it actually supports learning instead of replacing it.
